Welcome to Tennyson Education Centre

Who we are. Tennyson Education Centre is an Ofsted-registered after-school club and tuition centre in Small Heath, Birmingham, for children aged 8 to 16 (primary and secondary). Our sessions run Wednesday to Friday, 5:30 pm – 7:30 pm, providing tuition and enrichment in a safe, caring environment. We welcome all children regardless of gender, background, colour, race, religion or ethnicity.

Terms & Conditions, Privacy Policy & Content Usage

Content Usage

  • Purpose: Photos, videos, and interviews are used to celebrate student achievements, showcase our programmes, and promote Tennyson Education Centre's services.
  • Platforms: Content may be shared on social media (e.g. Facebook, Instagram, YouTube), the Tennyson Education Centre website, printed materials, and email newsletters.
  • Types of Media: Includes photographs, videos, and interviews with students or parents.
  • Consent Flexibility: Parents can consent to specific types of content (e.g. photos only) while declining others.
  • Safeguarding: Media will be reviewed to ensure it adheres to Tennyson Education Centre's safeguarding policies and portrays students positively. No personal details (e.g. full names) will be shared without explicit consent.
  • Duration of Use: Content may be used indefinitely unless consent is withdrawn.
  • Ownership: Tennyson Education Centre owns the rights to all media and will not sell or share it with third parties without consent.
  • Withdrawal of Consent: Consent can be withdrawn at any time by contacting tecentre@hotmail.com. Previously published content may remain online or in print.
  • No Impact on Participation: Consent decisions will not affect a student's participation in Tennyson Education Centre activities or services.

Terms and Conditions

1. Services and Schedule
  • Tennyson Education Centre provides after-school childcare and tuition in English, Maths, Science, SATs, 11+ and Grammar preparation (Key Stage 2 to GCSE) on Wednesday to Friday, 5:30 pm to 7:30 pm. Closure dates (e.g. school holidays) are communicated to parents in advance.
2. Fees
  • All fees are to be paid on a monthly basis.
  • Fees must be paid prior to joining the class for the upcoming month.
  • Delays in fees will result in suspension from Tennyson Education Centre.
3. Attendance and Punctuality
  • Parents must contact the site manager if a student will be late.
  • 48 hours' notice is required for student absences. Failure to provide notice will result in a full charge for the missed class.
  • Students should be dropped off no earlier than 10 minutes before class start time and collected no later than 10 minutes after class end time.
  • If a parent is more than 10 minutes late in collecting their child, a £5 late fee will be applied.
4. Stationery Requirements
  • Primary school children should bring a pencil, ruler, sharpener, and rubber.
  • Secondary school children should bring pens, a pencil, ruler, sharpener, rubber, trigonometry set, and calculator.
5. Academic Responsibilities
  • Students must complete their weekly packs and bring them to the centre the following week.
6. Behaviour and Discipline
  • Student behaviour must be excellent, with no disruptions allowed. We have a zero-tolerance policy for disruptive behaviour. Failure to comply will result in removal from the centre.
7. Mobile Phones
  • Tennyson Education Centre is a no-phone zone. Students are not to bring phones to the centre. Where an exception applies, mobile phones must be handed to the site manager before classes begin.
8. Enrolment and Registration
  • Parents/guardians must complete and sign an enrolment form prior to the student's first class.
  • All necessary student information, including emergency contact details, must be provided at the time of registration.
9. Health and Safety
  • Parents must inform the centre of any medical conditions or allergies the student has.
  • In case of illness or accident during class, parents will be notified immediately, and appropriate medical action will be taken.
10. Holiday and Closure Policies
  • Tennyson Education Centre operates Wednesday to Friday, 5:30 pm to 7:30 pm, except during advertised holiday closures.
  • In the event of unforeseen closures (e.g. extreme weather), classes will be rescheduled or refunded.
11. Refunds and Cancellations
  • No refunds will be given for missed classes without 48 hours' notice.
  • If Tennyson Education Centre needs to cancel a class, a make-up session will be arranged or a credit for the next month will be issued.
  • A 30-day notice is required if you intend to leave Tennyson Education Centre.
12. Parental Responsibilities
  • Parents are responsible for ensuring their children arrive on time and are collected promptly.
  • Parents must inform the centre if someone other than the designated individual will be picking up the student.
  • If a parent is more than 10 minutes late in collecting their child, a £5 late fee will be applied.
13. Code of Conduct
  • Students must respect teachers, staff, and fellow students.
  • Bullying or harassment of any kind will not be tolerated and will result in immediate expulsion.
14. Property Damage
  • Any damage to Tennyson Education Centre property caused by a student will be the financial responsibility of the parents/guardians.
15. Personal Belongings
  • Tennyson Education Centre is not responsible for lost or stolen items. Students are advised to leave valuable items at home.
16. Privacy Policy
  • All personal information provided will be kept confidential and used solely for administrative purposes, in compliance with UK GDPR.
17. Photographs and Videos
  • Tennyson Education Centre may take photographs or videos of students for promotional purposes. Parents must notify the centre in writing if they do not consent.
18. Communication
  • Tennyson Education Centre will communicate important information via email or phone. Parents are responsible for ensuring their contact details are up to date.
19. Emergency Procedures
  • Parents must familiarise themselves with the centre's emergency procedures and evacuation plans.
